## The Moggunu Conspiracy
During the [[Unification of the Isle of Prānta]], the [[Free (City-)States of Moggunu]] had, individually, funded the PGM war machine for the better part of twenty years, resulting in a large debt being held by the Moggunu cities (specifically the cities of [[Free (City-)States of Moggunu|Kanḍār, Remmimollu, and Vīḍugol]]). Post unification, fiscal advisers to [[Queen Māṣiḷu I of House Chārivi]] expressed adamant hope that they would be able to repay the debt, though it was fairly hefty, in a relatively short amount of time now that the resources of Pranta and Manki were uncontestedly theirs, and now that they had inherited the far-west trade routes from the West Prakāris. Further, the strong PGM navy was tasked with the protection of those far-west trade routes, meaning the PGM treasury could cut costs that used to be spent by the West Prakāris on private Moggunu-funded mercenary protection of those trade routes. 

What the PGM did not realise at the time was that the Moggunu states they were indebted to, the very same ones that funded, and in return were paid handsomely for, the private security for the trade routes, were angered by the PGM's actions of cutting costs. Moggunu officials were worried that a PGM that was not reliant on or indebted to Moggunu cities was one that would pose a grave threat to their economic power. Thus, it became the unified objective of the three aforementioned cities to undermine, where-ever possible, PGM economic independence. The three cities began funding piracy campaigns all around the waters of Pranta, causing mass pillaging, piracy, and instability on around the island. In one of Queen Māṣiḷu's [[Gunḍastanu Catāsu]] (Privy Council) meetings, the leader of the PGM navy, having spoken with political advisers, stated:
>"Our navy is strong enough to mostly keep the piracy at bay, and to continue our protection of all of our trade routes, from the far west, to the Moggunu cities. However, with the debt we owe we cannot build it up further nor can we redirect the navy's focus else where to realise our foreign objectives."
- [[Admiral of the Navy, Lekkula Damdyojamu]], 994 AW.

### Discovery of the Source of Piracy
After the PGM began repaying its debt to the three Moggunu city states, the military command deemed it necessary to embed undercover operatives into the political systems, governments, banks, and lenders of the city states as they desired to know of weaknesses that the PGM could play to its advantage against Moggunu.

During the piracy crisis (993 AW - 995 AW) that ravaged the island of Pranta, these same undercover assets finally obtained objective evidence of Moggunu involvement in the crisis, and a clear desire of wanting to undermine PGM sovereignty and economic independence. This was later dubbed by prominent Sōmyāmu (classical) historians (such as Amstaledi) as *[[The Piracy Document]]*. 
## The Pivot to Moggunu
Following the revelation of the Piracy Document, the [[Gunḍastanu Catāsu]] indefinitely pivoted PGM foreign policy to "support the independence and sovereignty of Mīsāḍinastu by undermining those who would seek to harm \[us\]", says *Amstaledi*. 

The Catāsu planned a multi-year long plan to deal with this situation. They would:
1. Increase undercover clandestine operations in the three debtor cities.
2. Attempt to play the other cities against these three, to overall weaken the free states.
3. Eventually, subjugate by influence or invasion.

It was attested that Māṣiḷu acknowledged the scale of this scheme and policy pivot may outlast many at that meeting, but reinforced the geopolitical weight of the situation, and the importance of PGM sovereignty.
