## Introduction
[[West Prakār]] (WP) and the [[Vāstuguku Pinnaganyāḍi/Prānta Gunḍastanāsu Mīsāḍinastuku (PGM).md|Prānta Gunḍastanāsu Mīsāḍinastuku]] (PGM) were two ancient independent kingdoms on the Isle of Pranta, attested from at least 100 AW. By 940 AW, both WP and PGM hold approximately half the island each -- the WP on the western half (owing to its name) and the PGM the eastern half. By ~993 AW, WP would cease to exist, having been fully annexed by the PGM.

For the almost 60 years that the two nations were the most predominant -- or only -- sovereign states on Pranta, they were almost totally at odds and tensions were mostly high. Their conflicts through the six decades included full, open war, funding anti-state actors, proxy wars, and clandestine operations.

## The Early Years (940 AW to 951 AW)
The domination of Pranta by these two kingdoms coincided with the rise of the Mīsāḍinian king *[[Maggayya II of House Chārivi]]*. A new leader, coupled with, what the Mīsāḍinians believed, a new existential threat caused an overhaul of Mīsāḍinastu's foreign policy. The old foreign policy, if there was one, was replaced with a new one -- one that would determine much of Mīsāḍinastu life, policy, and leadership for the decades to come.

Maggayya and his advisors in the early *[[Taṣṭinupuku Pinnaganyāḍi/Sōmyamu Mīsāḍinu/Nouns/āgrūmabayu.md|Agrūmbayu]]* determined that the only way for Mīsāḍinastu to prosper peacefully was for the full domination of Pranta, either by exacting a Mīsāḍinian sphere of influence (SOI), or by total annexation of the island. By the mid 940s AW, border skirmishes between GPM and WP had already occurred on numerous occasions, around the central mountainous area due to the discovery of extensive silver reserves there. These skirmishes resulted in the fortification of the full border by the WPs in the event of a full-scale war and invasion from the GPM.

When war broke out in 947 AW and the Mīsāḍinians determined that they could not break through West Prakāri fortifications, Mīsāḍinian military command advised Maggayya to begin commissioning a large naval fleet. Much of the West Prakāri economy relied on trade with the far west, and so a strong fleet would aid the GPM in the strangulation, aiming to force the eventual capitulation, of the WP economy.

	WP did not possess a dominant navy of their own, despite the reality of their trade, because for most of their known history, mercenary fleets funded by the Free (City-)States of Moggunu had vowed to protect WP trade with the far west, and in return WP could engage in tariff-free trade with the cities of Moggunu.

Over the course of the war, the GPM managed to blockade major West Prakāri ports and carry out naval pillages in settlements across the west Pranta coast, much to the WP's detriment. In response, West Prakāri land forces consistently advanced through western GPM in the border area, seizing swaths of land. 

However, the GPM economy had not, at that time, relied so greatly on maritime trade as the West Prakāris had, and the majority of the GPM economic area was located near the capital, Mīsāḍinastu, on the eastern coast of Pranta. The WPs tried many times to land naval forces on the eastern coast, but could not manage to break through the superior GPM navy. Eventually, the West Prakāri trade situation resulted in a huge recession and economic crisis and as such they were forced to negotiate for peace. Surprisingly, Maggayya did not impose any sort of financial burden upon West Prakār, but instead only took the central mountain range. Some primary historians suggest that he felt for the WPs that day due to the state of their economy, and so allowed a full withdrawal without imposing punitive reparations. Regardless, the GPM came out as the clear victor and stronger party in the war, with a superior navy and undisputed access to the new silver reserves. However, the classical history *Amstaledi* specifically states that calling Pranta under the influence of the GPM would absolutely be incorrect. The West Prakāri were broken, their economy in disrepair, but their land armies were still some of the best in the Prankita world and they would begin to bounce back relatively easily. **The war ended in mid-948 AW**.

## The Manki Conflict (951 AW - 952 AW)
With, what's now known as the [[Maggayya Policy]], in full swing, much of Mīsāḍinian statesmanship and policy revolved around the geopolitical chess-match that had started with the West Prakāris back in 940 AW. However, for the past two years the island enjoyed relatively low tensions as the WP military recovered and the nation began to stabilize its economy.

The next flashpoint began in 951 AW when the West Prakāri private company, [[Malbigya]] (owned by [[Bilchē Malbigya]]), discovered 'huge' polymetallic deposits of Tin and Silver on the small [[Island of Manki]], off the coast of western Pranta. Malbigya began to set up operations on the island, and contacted the WP king for military support and security -- with the assumption that WP would be more willing to pay higher prices. However, the Mīsāḍinian leadership had set up undercover assets within the company. Upon discovery of the deposits, these assets swiftly reported the situation back to the GPM military command and Maggayya II. In the days it took for the WP receive their report, and send military support, the Mīsāḍinian navy had dispatched a task force with, what would become, Mīsāḍinian marines to secure the island and the surrounding waters. Upon arrival, the task force arrested any members of the Malbigya company that refused to co-operate and began to force the rest to continue mining operations. 

Bilchē Malbigya, a native WP, was oft considered a neutral political man. He backed whichever side gave him money. the Mīsāḍinians managed to gain his early support swiftly, though, by offering greater incentives including tax holidays if he accepted their exclusive contract to provide them all the resources from Manki -- and, of course, a villa in central Mīsāḍinastu.

Upon arrival of the WP force, the king of West Prakār swiftly ordered the removal and withdrawal of all Mīsāḍinian personnel from the island stating they were meddling in internal WP affairs and had forced Malbigya and his people to work for them under duress. Bilchē Malbigya swiftly put an end to this idea by declaring his contract with the Mīsāḍinians. For 24 hours, the Mīsāḍinian task force and the West Prakāri naval forces stood on the brink of battle. **What hhappened then?**

With the withdrawal of West Prakāri forces from Manki, due to their recognised inferior naval capabilities, the WP king looked to alternatives. Most recently, a tribal group in the far north-west portion of the GPM was growing ever unsettled with the current Maggayya rule. This group, the *Kaḍimi Tribe*, considered themselves more ethnically aligned with West Prakār, however were content to remain under GPM rule under the condition that their interactions with West Prakāri border peoples would not be intruded upon. Thus, West Prakāri royal assets set out to sow distrust among the tribe regarding how well Maggayya would keep his promise. As such, it was not surprising when the tribe began to revolt and declare their independence from Maggayya's kingdom. It's important to note that this area was home to much of the GPM's lumber reserves. When mediations and diplomatic attempts between the tribe and the GPM, by GPM statesmen, failed, the WP had positioned themselves as the best mediators to end the rebellion -- due to their close ethnic ties to the tribe. Immediately the WP delegation consistently stoked the flames and made the crisis worse. 

It was at this point in the WP & GPM situation that multiple cities [[Free (City-)States of Moggunu]] began to silently fund all four sides in this conflict: the GPM, the WP, the growing private Malbigya militia, and the revolting tribe. The Moggunu city states had, for the better part of the past 50 years, been prospering largely due to their extensive trade outposts and colonies that dotted the known worlds' coast funnelling money back onto the island (the [[Island of Moggunu]]). They were best posed to make money from the constant conflicts in Pranta. As such, both the WP and GPM began a large military build-up with the new financial backing of Moggunu. 

Manki became a central flashpoint once again in 952 AW when the Malbigya company announced the switch of the exclusive 'Manki mining contract' to West Prakār. The Mīsāḍinians were furious at the lack of warning and the betrayal, and as such landed marines onto the island within the day (as the Manki task force was still patrolling the area, to dissuade the West Prakāri navy). The marines held skirmishes and pitched battles with the Malbigya militia and eventually managed to seize the island and Company operations within the week. By this point, West Prakāri forces had arrived at the island had had begun to engage in fleet actions with the Mīsāḍinian naval task force.

## The Conflict Comes to a Head (952 AW - 954 AW)
After the battles of Manki in 952 AW, conflict broke out all over Pranta as the West Prakāris officially announced a formal alliance with the Kaḍimi Tribe in the following days. The border erupted in multiple skirmishes, with the West Prakāris beginning an unrelenting advance. 

Mīsāḍinians, despite struggling to push them back or to outright halt the advances, managed to at least ensure the the advance was slow enough to allow their superior navy to strike back. The navy, in 950 AW, had developed the "cammusāgupu" (quinqueremes) in secret. Up until this point, Pranta's navies had only used the ruvasāgu (trireme). The new cammusāgu allowed Mīsāḍinu to ram with more power, and to transport a higher number of marines on a warship, as well as on a typical transport vessel. They began to use this, in what they called a "Cammusāgu Talevi Pinṭāgu" (CTP). Using this, and marine experience gained previously, the PGM began extensive fleet actions on the southern coast of West Prakār, and pushed inland with their marines with little initial resistance, as most WP land forces were focused on the border. With the redeployment of many forces, the defending Mīsāḍinu armies gained some slight breathing room, while the marines continued their advancement towards the central Pranta lake, even while facing ever-more fierce WP resistance.

Once the marines had arrived at and seized the central lake, the West Prakāri forces that had advanced through the southern portion of the border, south of the central mountain range, was forced to pull back lest they get encircled. The majority of southern West Prakār was now under Mīsāḍinastu's control. Mīsāḍinu focused on fortifying the current border with the WP forces pushing through the north, so that they could focus on continuing their marine-spearheaded push from the central lake through West Prakār.

## The Subjugation of West Prakār (954 AW - 970 AW)
Eventually, the PGM continued their push through WP. The WP managed to continue their push into the Mīsāḍinu homeland and break through their fortifications, before being routed in the [[Battle of Kēmiṣa]] in 954 AW.

The WP state eventually collapsed under the weight of constant push-back, and due to economic crises that followed from the GPM navy blockading major ports again (the WP continued their reliance on maritime trade whilst also not bolstering their navy in the decades after the initial war, it seems).

Much of the West Prakāri land was annexed by the PGM, leaving only the northern portion as a semi-autonomous puppet state that was reliant on Mīsāḍinu for economic aid. The Kaḍimi Tribe swiftly ended their revolt now, citing the reasoning as an "end to the conflict and refound trust in the Mīsāḍinu state". 

The Valarian historical economist Maximus Felix Syllogikó later analysed the financial gains of the city states of Moggunu from this decades-long conflict, concluding that they, on average, almost doubled their wealth from funding all sides.

### Aftermath
In the aftermath of the subjugation (and annexation of southern WP), the PGM government, supported by the Agrūmabayu, opted to grant every person in the newly subjugated territory PGM citizenship automatically. Further, Maggayya decreed that West Prakāri citizenship would be declared equal to PGM citizenship, allowing both Mīsāḍinians and West Prakāris to work or live in both nations, vote, and serve in the military. Furthermore, PGM pressured the WP puppet monarch to dissolve the WP military (army and navy), and merge all units and assets into the Mīsāḍinian military -- officially renamed to the "Pranta Military" in order to promote island unity and peace.

The reasoning for such swift and heavy integration, as Amstaledi writes, was likely due to the fact that, culturally, the  viewed the West Prakāri culture as essentially the same as its own, with only slight variations. In fact, in Amstaledi's time, *West Prakāri* was (and still is, up to a lesser extent) mutually intelligible with Mīsāḍinu. They both felt as though they were the same people, linguistically, ethnically, and culturally.

In fact, in just four generations' time, the West Prakāris would be calling themselves "Citizens of Mīsāḍinu" (as attested in the *Kamḍa Scroll*) and their language would officially be decreed as an equal dialect of the Mīsāḍinu language. 

### Eventual Annexation of the Isle of Pranta (993 AW)
Pranta was eventually totally annexed by Mīsāḍinu in 993 AW due to the scandal of a subsequent  West Prakāri puppet king (unknown, and unnamed). The state descended into turmoil, and West Prakāri elites begged the Mīsāḍinian queen, [[Queen Māṣiḷu I of House Chārivi]] (daughter of Maggayya II) to intervene militarily.
